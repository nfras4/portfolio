Texture options // four candidates, no sub-pixel noise paper grain rejected · these are the alternatives
Pick a texture, or stay with control.
A · lattice bg
01about

A 28px dot grid at low opacity.

Direct extension of the brand mark. Reads as "this product is in a measured grid." Most coherent with the lattice DNA you already use everywhere. Dot color is dim ink at 32% alpha.

A · lattice bg · 28px radial-gradient grid, dim ink @ 32% alpha. Pro: matches your brand mark. Con: visible enough to count as a pattern, not invisible texture.
B · grid paper
02about

Engineering notebook ruling.

Crosshatch of 1px hairlines forming 48px squares. Already in use on your project image placeholders. Promoting it page-wide makes the whole site feel like a draft sheet under the content.

B · grid paper · 48px linear-gradient crosshatch, border @ 50% alpha. Pro: ties to existing placeholder style. Con: strongest of the four; might fight the type.
C · diagonal hatch
03about

45° hatching, very faint.

Repeating linear-gradient at 45°, 8px pitch, sub-pixel line width, low opacity. Reads more as "atmosphere" than as a pattern. Stable on scroll, no moiré because the lines are regular and aligned to pixels.

C · diagonal hatch · repeating-linear-gradient 45°, 8px pitch, 18% alpha. Pro: invisible-feeling. Con: still texture-y; might also feel funny if grain did.
D · column rules
04about

Two vertical hairlines, marking the content gutter.

Not a texture. Two 1px verticals at the left and right edges of the 1180px shell, running the full page height. Reads as ruled paper margins. Structural, not decorative. Zero pattern energy.

D · column rules · two 1px lines at shell edges, fixed bg. Pro: most disciplined, feels like the rest of your system. Con: not really texture; if you want surface interest, this won't add it.
ctrl · no texture

ctrl · background: var(--bg). Nothing layered. Lowest noise, also the current default.
